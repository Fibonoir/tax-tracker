## Design Context

### Users

Italian freelance professionals in `regime forfettario`. They use the app to answer one practical question quickly: how much money is actually usable after taxes, INPS, and expected costs. They are not looking for accounting theater or a full ERP. They need fast confidence, low ceremony, and numbers they can trust.

### Brand Personality

Clear, grounded, modern.

The product should feel calm, credible, and financially literate. It should reduce anxiety rather than create excitement for its own sake. The tone should be direct and human, never bureaucratic, never startup-generic.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a modern finance aesthetic with blue-sky accents, paper-white light surfaces, and dark-slate dark mode surfaces. Prefer restrained depth, strong typography, and clean card architecture over decorative gradients. The UI should feel like a precise financial notebook, not a neon SaaS dashboard.

Anti-direction:

- no green-led visual identity
- no heavy atmospheric gradients
- no generic growth-hacker dashboard look
- no long, ceremonial onboarding

### Design Principles

1. Optimize for decision speed over feature exposure.
2. Keep the most important number visually dominant on every key screen.
3. Put advanced fiscal detail in settings, not in the first-run flow.
4. Use color to support clarity and trust, not spectacle.
5. Make every screen feel lighter, calmer, and more exact than the current version.
